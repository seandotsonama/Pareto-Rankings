What is Pareto Ranking?

This ranking uses a thresholded dominance model rather than simple averaging. It identifies which candidates are meaningfully stronger across categories without inventing weights or assuming equal importance.

For each category, the standard deviation across all candidate scores is computed. The dominance threshold for that category is set at 0.5 × stddev. Differences smaller than this threshold are treated as neutral — not wins or losses. This prevents noise and minor score gaps from driving the ranking.

Candidate A strongly dominates Candidate B if A has at least one category win against B, zero category losses, and more wins than losses. A candidate in Tier 1 has no strong losses and the highest net dominance score. Tier 2 candidates have mixed profiles. Tier 3 candidates are more often dominated than dominant.

This is not a weighted average model. It does not assume equal importance across categories. Small differences inside the threshold band are explicitly ignored.

Example: A scores [90, 80, 70, 85, 88, 76] and B scores [88, 79, 68, 84, 85, 74].
A is higher on every metric → A dominates B → B drops to Tier 2.

But if B scores [95, 79, 68, 84, 85, 74], B beats A on Charisma.
Neither fully dominates the other → both land in Tier 1.
